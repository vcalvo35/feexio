Stripe vs. Square vs. PayPal:
Fee Comparison for Small Businesses

📅 April 23, 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read ✍️ Victor A. Calvo S.
Last updated: April 2026  |  Disclaimer

Stripe, Square, and PayPal are the three most widely used payment processors for independent sellers, freelancers, and small businesses. Each has strengths in different contexts — online sales, in-person POS, invoicing — and the "best" choice depends entirely on how and where you sell. Here's a clear side-by-side breakdown.

Online Card Transactions

  • Stripe: 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card transaction. International cards add 1.5%. Currency conversion adds 1%.
  • Square: 2.9% + $0.30 per online card transaction. International cards accepted at the same rate (Square handles conversion internally).
  • PayPal: 2.9% + $0.30 for standard transactions. International adds 1.5%–2%. Currency conversion: 3–4% spread (the most expensive of the three).

Winner for online sales: Stripe (best international rates) or Square (if you want a single rate for all cards).

In-Person / Point of Sale (POS)

  • Stripe: 2.7% + $0.05 per swipe/tap/chip transaction. Requires Stripe Terminal hardware ($59–$299).
  • Square: 2.6% + $0.10 per swipe/chip/tap transaction. Free card reader included. Best ecosystem for physical retail.
  • PayPal: 2.29% + $0.09 per transaction with Zettle (PayPal's in-person product). Card reader required ($29–$79).

Winner for in-person: Square (lower rate, free hardware, best POS software) or PayPal Zettle (lowest rate but limited features).

Invoicing

  • Stripe: Same as online rate (2.9% + $0.30). Invoicing is available via Stripe Invoicing — free for manual invoices, $0.40/invoice for automatic billing.
  • Square: Same as online rate (2.9% + $0.30) for card payments on invoices. Square Invoices is free to use.
  • PayPal: 2.9% + $0.30 for invoices paid by card via PayPal. Invoicing tool is free and well-suited for freelancers with no technical setup required.

Winner for invoicing: PayPal (most familiar to clients and easiest to use for one-off freelance invoices) or Square (if you need more structured invoicing workflows).

ACH / Bank Transfers

  • Stripe: 0.8% capped at $5.00 — significantly cheaper for large transactions.
  • Square: Not available natively.
  • PayPal: Free for PayPal balance transfers between US accounts. ACH with bank not directly available via standard Invoicing.

Winner for large transactions: Stripe ACH. On a $5,000 invoice, Stripe ACH costs $5 vs. $145.30 for a card transaction.

Developer Experience & Integration

  • Stripe: Industry-leading APIs, documentation, and ecosystem. The choice for developers building custom payment flows, SaaS subscriptions, or marketplace billing.
  • Square: Good APIs, strong POS SDK. Best for businesses with both online and in-person channels.
  • PayPal: Widely recognized by consumers, easy "buy now" buttons, PayPal checkout button increases conversions in some markets. APIs are functional but less elegant than Stripe.

Which Should You Choose?

  • Freelancers invoicing clients: PayPal for familiarity; Stripe if you need ACH for large invoices.
  • Online store (no in-person): Stripe for its superior international and developer tooling.
  • Physical retail or food service: Square — its POS ecosystem is unmatched at this tier.
  • Hybrid (online + in-person): Square or Stripe with Terminal.
  • SaaS or subscription business: Stripe — nothing else comes close for recurring billing infrastructure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Stripe or Square cheaper for online transactions?

For standard US domestic card transactions, both charge 2.9% + $0.30 — identical. The difference emerges for international payments (Stripe is cheaper at 1.5% extra vs. Square's flat rate) and large bank transfers (Stripe's ACH at 0.8% capped at $5 is far cheaper than card processing for large invoices).

Does Square work for online-only businesses?

Yes. Square Online is a viable e-commerce platform with the same 2.9% + $0.30 processing rate. However, Square's real strength is in physical retail and hybrid businesses. For pure online/digital businesses, Stripe typically offers more flexibility and better developer tools.

Which payment processor has the lowest fees overall?

It depends on the transaction type. For online card payments, all three charge ~2.9% + $0.30. For in-person, Square and PayPal Zettle are cheaper than Stripe Terminal. For large bank transfers, Stripe ACH (0.8%, max $5) is dramatically cheaper than any card-based option on all three platforms.
