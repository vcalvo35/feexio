Wholesale Purchasing and Logistics Optimization Optimización de Compras al por Mayor y Logística

📅 April 5, 2026⏱️ 6 min read✍️ Victor A. Calvo S.
Last updated: April 2026  |  Disclaimer

Buying wholesale seems cheaper — but the real equation includes variables that the unit price doesn't show: storage costs, occupied space, inventory obsolescence, and if the supplier is international, the unit conversion costs that affect volume calculations.

Comprar al por mayor parece siempre más barato, pero la ecuación real incluye variables que el precio por unidad no muestra: el costo de almacenamiento, el espacio ocupado, la obsolescencia del inventario y, si el proveedor es internacional, los costos de conversión de unidades que afectan el cálculo de volumen.

This is especially useful when comparing two suppliers offering the same material in different-sized containers. The unit price of Supplier A may be lower, but if their packaging occupies more cubic space per the same volume of product, Supplier B is actually more efficient in terms of real cost per volume.

Esto es especialmente útil cuando comparas entre dos proveedores que ofrecen el mismo material en contenedores de distintos tamaños. Puede ser que el precio por unidad del proveedor A sea menor, pero si su empaque ocupa más espacio cúbico por el mismo volumen de producto, el proveedor B resulta más eficiente en términos de costo por volumen real.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I compare suppliers from different countries?

Calculate the total landed cost per unit for each supplier: product price + shipping + import duties + payment processing fees + currency conversion costs. A supplier with a lower unit price but higher shipping, duties, or 4% international payment fee may cost more per unit delivered than a slightly pricier supplier with lower logistics costs. Use a spreadsheet or Feexio's Cost per Unit Calculator to run the comparison.

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) threshold worth negotiating?

Most manufacturers offer better unit prices at 2–3× their stated MOQ. Before committing to a large order, calculate whether the price reduction covers your storage, cash flow, and obsolescence risk. A 15% unit price reduction is less attractive if you have 6 months of unsold inventory tying up capital. Calculate the break-even sell-through rate before increasing your order size.

How do I account for volume units when comparing suppliers?

Convert all quantities to a common unit — kg, L, or units — before comparing prices. International suppliers often quote in different volume standards (gallons vs liters, lbs vs kg). Use SwiftConvertHub's free unit converter to standardize measurements across quotes. A $2.50/kg quote from one supplier and a $1.10/lb quote from another require conversion before comparison.
