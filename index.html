Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about fees, rates and financial calculations.

How does PayPal calculate its fees in 2025?

PayPal charges a standard rate of 3.49% + $0.49 per transaction for goods and services payments sent from a PayPal balance or bank account. Payments made with a credit or debit card are charged at 3.49% + $0.49 as well. For merchants using PayPal Checkout, the rate is typically 3.49% + $0.49 per transaction. Our calculator uses the most common rate of 3.49% + $0.49. Always verify with PayPal's official pricing page for your specific account type and country.

What is the difference between profit margin and markup?

Profit margin is calculated as (Profit ÷ Selling Price) × 100, while markup is calculated as (Profit ÷ Cost Price) × 100. For example, if an item costs $60 and sells for $100, the profit is $40. The profit margin is 40% but the markup is 66.7%. Margin is always lower than markup for the same transaction. Businesses typically use margin to evaluate profitability and markup to set prices.

How much does Fiverr take from sellers in 2025?

Fiverr charges sellers a flat 20% service fee on every order, regardless of the order value. This means if a buyer pays $100 for your gig, you receive $80. The 20% fee applies to the base order amount, tips, and any extra services (gig extras). There is no tiered system — the rate stays at 20% whether you earn $5 or $5,000. Our Fiverr fee calculator lets you instantly see your net earnings after Fiverr's cut.

How do I calculate a fair freelance hourly rate?

Start by determining your desired annual income and add your monthly business expenses (software, equipment, taxes, health insurance). Divide the total annual cost by the number of billable hours you realistically work per year — typically 1,000 to 1,400 hours for a full-time freelancer. For example, if you need $5,000/month and have $500 in expenses, and bill 80 hours/month, your minimum rate is $68.75/hour. Use our Freelance Rate Calculator to run this calculation instantly.

What is Stripe's processing fee and how is it applied?

Stripe's standard processing fee is 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card charge in the United States. For manually entered cards, the rate increases to 3.4% + $0.30. International cards add an additional 1.5% fee. Stripe does not charge monthly fees, setup fees, or minimum transaction fees. The fee is automatically deducted from each payment before it is deposited into your bank account. Our Stripe Fee Calculator shows you the exact net amount you receive after fees.
