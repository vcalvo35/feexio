How PayPal Fees Work in 2026

PayPal charges 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction for standard goods and services payments in the US. The percentage applies to the transaction amount, and the fixed $0.30 fee is added on top. For a $100 payment, you receive $96.80 after fees ($100 × 2.9% = $2.90 + $0.30 = $3.20 total fee).

Different PayPal payment types carry different rates. Manually entered payments, international transactions, and PayPal Here (card readers) all use separate fee schedules. The calculator above uses the standard Goods & Services rate that applies to most online payments.

How to Price Your Work to Cover PayPal Fees

To receive a specific net amount after PayPal fees, divide your target amount by (1 - 0.029), then add $0.30. For example, to receive exactly $100.00: $100 ÷ 0.971 = $102.99, plus $0.30 = $103.09 gross invoice amount.

Many freelancers and sellers add a flat 3–4% to their prices to offset PayPal fees, or include a line item in their invoices that reads "Payment processing fee." Always check PayPal's official rates for your country — rates vary internationally.

PayPal Friends & Family vs Goods & Services

PayPal Friends & Family (F&F) transfers have no fee for the sender or receiver — but they provide zero buyer or seller protection. Using F&F for business transactions violates PayPal's terms of service and can result in account suspension. Always use Goods & Services for business payments, even if it means paying the fee. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the PayPal fee for a $100 payment?

PayPal charges 2.9% + $0.30 for standard Goods & Services payments. On a $100 payment, the fee is $3.20 (2.9% × $100 = $2.90 + $0.30), leaving you with $96.80.

How do I avoid PayPal fees?

You can't eliminate PayPal fees on business payments, but you can offset them by adding 3-4% to your invoice total, using PayPal's invoice tool (which supports automatic fee pass-through), or using alternative payment methods like bank transfer for large transactions.

Are PayPal fees different for international payments?

Yes. International payments add a currency conversion fee of 3-4% on top of the standard transaction fee. The total cost for an international payment can reach 6-8% of the transaction amount.

Does PayPal charge fees for receiving money from friends?

Friends & Family (F&F) transfers are free within the same country when funded from a PayPal balance or bank account. However, F&F is prohibited for business transactions and provides no buyer or seller protection.
