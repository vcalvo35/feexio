Una URL rastreada en tu menú digital te permite medir cuántas veces se abre cada sección. Si tienes diferentes páginas para entrantes, platos principales y postres, puedes usar parámetros UTM distintos para cada sección y descubrir qué parte del menú recibe más atención.

Este dato de comportamiento digital es valioso, pero incompleto si no se cruza con la rentabilidad real de cada plato.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do QR codes help with menu profitability?

QR codes with UTM parameters let you track which menu items get the most views digitally. When you cross that engagement data with your profit margin per dish, you can identify high-margin items that deserve more prominence and low-margin items that get too much attention. It turns menu design from guesswork into a data-driven decision.

What tools do I need to set this up?

You need three things: a QR code generator (free tools like InstantLinkHub work well), UTM parameter tracking through Google Analytics or a similar tool, and a profit margin calculation for each menu item using Feexio's Profit Margin Calculator. The setup takes a few hours and gives you ongoing insight into menu performance.

How often should I update my QR menu and pricing?

Review pricing every 6 months minimum, or whenever ingredient costs shift significantly (more than 10%). Use QR codes to push updates instantly — no printing required. Track whether price changes affect order volumes on specific items through your analytics, and adjust margins accordingly. Digital menus make pricing experiments much cheaper than printed ones.
